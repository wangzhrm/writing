Countable and Uncountable Nouns

This page summarizes the usage of different words and expressions with countable and uncountable nouns, followed by a quiz to test your understanding.

Summary of Usage

For Countable Nouns

The number of: Used to refer to a specific, quantifiable quantity of people or things.
Example: **The number of** students in the class is 25.

Many: Refers to a large quantity.
Example: **Many** books were sold at the fair.

Few: Refers to a small, inadequate quantity.
Example: **Few** people attended the meeting.

Several: Refers to a small but indefinite number, more than two but not many.
Example: I bought **several** new shirts.

Each: Refers to every one of two or more people or things, regarded individually.
Example: **Each** participant received a certificate.

Numerous: Refers to a large number.
Example: The website has **numerous** positive reviews.

A great many: Emphasizes a very large number.
Example: **A great many** species of birds migrate in the winter.

For Uncountable Nouns

The amount of: Used to refer to a quantity of a substance or concept that cannot be counted.
Example: **The amount of** water needed for the recipe is 1 liter.

Much: Refers to a large quantity or amount.
Example: There isn't **much** information on the topic.

Little: Refers to a small, inadequate quantity or amount.
Example: There is **little** hope for a quick solution.

For Both Countable and Uncountable Nouns

Some: Refers to an unspecified number or amount.
Examples: There are **some** apples in the bowl. / I need **some** advice.

A lot of / Lots of: Refers to a large quantity or number.
Examples: **A lot of** cars were on the road. / We had **lots of** fun.

Plenty of: Refers to a sufficient or more than sufficient quantity or number.
Examples: We have **plenty of** chairs for everyone. / There's **plenty of** time.

Quiz

1. There were ______ people at the concert.

Correct Answer: C. a great many
Explanation: 'People' is a plural countable noun, and 'a great many' is used to emphasize a large number of countable nouns.

2. We need to find a house with ______ of space.

Correct Answer: B. a great deal of
Explanation: 'Space' is an uncountable noun, and 'a great deal of' is used to refer to a large quantity of something uncountable.

3. I have ______ new ideas for the project.

Correct Answer: B. a lot of
Explanation: 'A lot of' can be used with both countable ('ideas') and uncountable nouns. The other options are only for uncountable nouns.

4. There is ______ water left in the bottle.

Correct Answer: B. little
Explanation: 'Water' is an uncountable noun. 'Little' is used for a small amount of an uncountable noun.

5. He has ______ friends in the city.

Correct Answer: B. few
Explanation: 'Friends' is a plural countable noun. 'Few' is used for a small number of countable nouns.

6. The company faced ______ problems during the transition.

Correct Answer: A. numerous
Explanation: 'Problems' is a plural countable noun. 'Numerous' is used for a large number of countable nouns.

7. The project requires a significant ______ of effort.

Correct Answer: B. amount
Explanation: 'Effort' is an uncountable noun, so 'amount' is the correct choice.

8. How ______ money do you need for the trip?

Correct Answer: C. much
Explanation: 'Money' is an uncountable noun. 'Much' is used in questions and negative sentences with uncountable nouns.

9. There are only ______ chairs left in the room.

Correct Answer: C. a few
Explanation: 'Chairs' is a plural countable noun. 'A few' is used for a small, but positive number of countable nouns.

10. ______ of the information was outdated.

Correct Answer: B. A lot
Explanation: 'Information' is an uncountable noun. 'A lot' (or 'lots') can be used with both countable and uncountable nouns.

11. There has been very ______ progress on the new project.

Correct Answer: B. little
Explanation: 'Progress' is an uncountable noun. 'Little' is used for a small amount of an uncountable noun.

12. ______ of the students passed the exam.

Correct Answer: A. Many
Explanation: 'Students' is a plural countable noun. 'Many' is used for a large number of countable nouns.

13. We need to save ______ time to finish this task.

Correct Answer: A. a lot of
Explanation: 'Time' is an uncountable noun. 'A lot of' can be used with both countable and uncountable nouns.

14. The police received ______ new leads on the case.

Correct Answer: C. several
Explanation: 'Leads' is a plural countable noun. 'Several' is used for a small but indefinite number of countable nouns.

15. There is ______ traffic on the roads today.

Correct Answer: B. less
Explanation: 'Traffic' is an uncountable noun. 'Less' is used for a smaller amount of an uncountable noun.

16. ______ people know the answer to this difficult question.

Correct Answer: A. Few
Explanation: 'People' is a plural countable noun. 'Few' is used for a small, inadequate number of countable nouns.

17. We have ______ fresh fruit left in the kitchen.

Correct Answer: A. plenty of
Explanation: 'Fruit' can be used as an uncountable noun in this context (referring to the category). 'Plenty of' can be used with both countable and uncountable nouns.

18. A lot of energy ______ wasted every day.

Correct Answer: B. is
Explanation: 'Energy' is an uncountable noun, which always takes a singular verb.

19. The project involved a great ______ of research.

Correct Answer: B. deal
Explanation: 'Research' is an uncountable noun. The correct expression is 'a great deal of'.

20. The factory produces ______ cars per year.

Correct Answer: C. a large number of
Explanation: 'Cars' is a plural countable noun. The correct expression for a large quantity of countable nouns is 'a large number of'.
