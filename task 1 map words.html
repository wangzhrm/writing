IELTS Task 1: Map Essential Language & Vocabulary

Map reports require verbs of development, nouns related to urban planning, and precise prepositions for location.

A. Describing Location and Direction

Use these prepositions and phrases to clearly position features relative to the entire map or other objects.

Function Expression Examples
Compass Directions to the north of, in the south-east corner, the area to the west
Relative Position adjacent to, next to, opposite, across from, on the border of
Central/Edge in the centre, in the heart of the town, on the outskirts, in the periphery
Fixed Position situated on, located at, running parallel to (for roads/rivers)

B. Describing Development and Change (Verbs)

Use Past Simple/Passive for historical changes and Future/Present Passive for development plans.

Category Past Tense Example (e.g., in 2000)
Adding/Creating The complex was constructed, was built, was established, was erected.
Removing/Clearing The forest was cleared, was demolished, was knocked down, was removed.
Modifying/Upgrading The facility was modernized, was refurbished, was converted, was expanded.
Relocation/Purpose The market was relocated to, was transferred, a new road was laid to provide access.

C. Key Nouns and Phrases for Urban Planning

These nouns and phrases help you vary your vocabulary when describing different areas and structures.

Category Noun/Phrase Examples
Residential Areas housing estate, residential complex, dwellings, accommodation
Commercial/Facilities retail outlet, leisure amenities, shopping centre, educational institution
Transport/Access pedestrianized area, road network, transport link, bypass, car park
Land Use/Nature farmland, green space, industrial zone, woodland, open space
