IELTS Writing Task 1: Introduction Paraphrasing Guide

Original Task Prompt (Process Diagram Example):

The diagram below shows how geothermal energy is used to produce electricity.

The introduction must be a single, concise sentence that restates the prompt in your own words. Here are three reliable techniques to achieve a high-scoring paraphrase:

1. Simple Synonym Replacement

Replace 2 to 4 key nouns and verbs with appropriate alternatives. This is the most straightforward technique.

  • Shows → illustrates, depicts, outlines, details
  • Diagram → figure, schematic, flow chart, image
  • Produce electricity → generate power, create electrical energy, electricity generation
Example: The figure illustrates the way in which electrical power is generated using geothermal resources.

2. Structural Rephrasing ("The process of...")

Change the focus from "what the diagram shows" to "the procedure itself." You can change the clause "how X does Y" to a noun phrase like "the procedure/stages for doing Y."

  • "Shows how..." → "Details the various stages involved in..."
  • "Used to produce..." → "Harnessed for the creation of..."
Example: The flow chart details the various stages involved in the conversion of underground heat into electrical current.

3. Voice or Grammar Change

Change the active or semi-active voice of the original into a passive structure (or use nominalization, converting verbs into nouns, like *produce* → *production*).

  • Original: (Geothermal energy) is used to produce (electricity).
  • Passive focus: (Electricity) is generated by harnessing (geothermal resources).
Example: The schematic depicts the entire procedure through which electricity is generated from deep geothermal heat sources.

Remember: Your introduction should be just one or two sentences and must not include any specific details from the body of the report (e.g., number of stages, specific temperatures, etc.).
