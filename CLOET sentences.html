IELTS Writing Task 1: Sentence Structures

A collection of useful sentence structures for each part of the CLOET method.

C - Change

These structures help describe the most significant overall change from the beginning to the end of the period.

Upward Trend

The most significant change was the dramatic increase in (category).
There was a noticeable trend towards a rise in (category) over the period.
Overall, the data indicates a clear shift from (initial state) to (final state).
(Category) experienced a rapid surge, reaching a peak of (value) by the end of the period.
The figures for (category) climbed steadily throughout the years, showing a consistent upward trajectory.
Following an initial dip, there was a sharp recovery, and the numbers for (category) grew substantially.

Downward Trend

The most striking feature is the considerable decline in (category).
There was a noticeable trend towards a fall in (category) over the period.
The data shows a downward trajectory for (category), with its value dropping from (value) to (value).
The numbers for (category) plummeted over the ten-year period, experiencing a significant decrease.
(Category) saw a steady decrease, falling from (initial value) in (year) to (final value) in (year).

Fluctuations

The data for (category) was volatile, experiencing several rises and falls over the period.
There were significant fluctuations in the number of (category), which peaked in (year) and then fell.
The figures for (category) fluctuated considerably, with no clear overall trend.

No Change

The figures for (category) remained relatively stable over the entire period.
There was little to no change in the number of (category) from (year) to (year).

L - Lowest & Highest

Use these sentences to highlight the highest and lowest points of the data.

(Category) consistently recorded the highest figures, while (another category) had the lowest.
The peak was reached by (category) in (year), whereas the lowest point was seen in (another category).
At (a specific point), (category) accounted for the largest proportion, in contrast to (another category) which had the smallest.
The highest number of (item) was seen in (category), at (value).
By (year), (category) had reached its lowest point of (value).
The chart shows that (category) was the most popular choice, while (another category) was the least.

O - Obvious Similarities & Differences

These structures are useful for comparing and contrasting different categories.

Both (category A) and (category B) followed a similar (upward/downward) trend.
In contrast to the (rise/fall) in (category A), (category B) saw a steady (opposite trend).
A key similarity is that (category A) and (category B) both experienced a decline, but the rate of decrease for (category A) was much more pronounced.
Unlike (category A), which rose steadily, (category B) fluctuated significantly over the period.
The trends for (category A) and (category B) were diametrically opposed, with the former rising and the latter falling.

E - Exceptional Categories

These sentences help to draw attention to any data points that are notably different from the rest.

One clear outlier is (category), which demonstrated a far more significant (change) than any other.
Interestingly, the trend for (category) was an exception to the general pattern, as it (description of change).
Unlike the other categories, (category) saw a consistent rise throughout the entire period.
What stands out is that (category) did not follow the general trend, instead remaining relatively stable.
Notably, the data for (category) was unique as it was the only one to show a sharp increase at the very end of the period.

T - Trend Consistency

Use these structures to describe whether a trend is consistent or if it changes over time.

The growth of (category) was slow and steady until (year), when it began to accelerate sharply.
There was a general downward trend for (category), albeit with some minor fluctuations.
Following a period of volatility, the figures for (category) stabilized in the latter half of the period.
The trend for (category) was largely consistent over the period, showing a gradual increase without any major drops.
Although there was an overall increase, the growth for (category) slowed down significantly after (year).
