IELTS Writing Task 1: Paraphrasing Introduction

In IELTS Writing Task 1, you must paraphrase the given task question's introduction to create your own opening sentence. This skill is vital for achieving a good score. Below are ten common examples. Try to paraphrase them yourself before clicking the button to see the suggested answers.

Practice Sentences

1. The chart below shows the number of new homes built per year in a specific area between 2000 and 2010.
2. The line graph compares the percentage of total sales made by three different shoe companies from 2005 to 2015.
3. The pie charts illustrate the proportion of five different types of household waste in one country in 2000 and 2015.
4. The table provides information about student enrolment in three different universities from 2005 to 2010.
5. The bar chart shows the percentage of people in a town who used public transport over a twenty-year period.
6. The graph below shows the amount of coffee consumed in four different countries from 1990 to 2005.
7. The chart shows the different reasons why people travel to work in a particular city.
8. The diagram below illustrates the process of making electricity from waste materials.
9. The map shows the changes to a park area between 2000 and the present day.
10. The table shows the changes in the number of people who visited three museums between 1999 and 2009.

Suggested Paraphrases

Method 1: Change the Structure

  • Example 1 Original: The chart below shows the number of new homes built per year...
  • Paraphrase: The chart illustrates the yearly construction of new residences...
  • Explanation: Reversing the order of noun phrases ("number of homes built" vs. "construction of homes") is a simple and effective technique.

Method 2: Use Synonyms

  • Example 2 Original: The line graph compares the percentage of total sales made by three different shoe companies...
  • Paraphrase: The line graph compares the proportion of overall revenue generated by three distinct footwear companies...
  • Explanation: Substituting words like 'compares' (compares), 'percentage' (proportion), 'sales' (revenue), 'different' (distinct), and 'companies' (firms) is a common strategy.

Method 3: Combine Methods

  • Example 3 Original: The pie charts illustrate the proportion of five different types of household waste in one country in 2000 and 2015.
  • Paraphrase: The two pie charts demonstrate how the composition of domestic refuse changed in a country over a 15-year period, from 2000 to 2015.
  • Explanation: This combines structural changes (from "in 2000 and 2015" to "over a 15-year period") with synonyms ('illustrate' to 'demonstrate', 'proportion' to 'composition', 'household waste' to 'domestic refuse').

Additional Paraphrase Examples:

  • Example 4: The table presents data on the total number of students enrolled in three academic institutions between 2005 and 2010.
  • Example 5: The bar chart demonstrates the percentage of the population using public transit in a town over a two-decade span.
  • Example 6: The given graph highlights the amount of coffee consumed in four nations between 1990 and 2005.
  • Example 7: The provided chart breaks down the various motives for commuting to work in a specific city.
  • Example 8: The diagram depicts the process by which electricity is generated from waste materials.
  • Example 9: The map outlines the developments and alterations that occurred in a park between 2000 and today.
  • Example 10: The table outlines how the number of visitors to three museums varied over the period of ten years, from 1999 to 2009.
