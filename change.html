IELTS Writing: Describing Trends

In IELTS Academic Writing Task 1, you need to describe and summarize trends in a graph or chart. Using a variety of vocabulary for increase, decrease, and stability is crucial for a high score. This guide will help you master the key words and phrases.

1. Vocabulary for an Increase

Verbs

These verbs are used to describe an upward movement in data.

  • increase: Sales increased in the last quarter.
  • rise: The price of oil rose steadily.
  • grow: The population grew by 10% in a decade.
  • climb: House prices have been climbing since 2010.
  • rocket: The stock market rocketed to an all-time high. (Dramatic)
  • soar: Profits soared after the marketing campaign. (Dramatic)

Nouns

These nouns are used after articles or prepositions (e.g., there was a significant increase).

  • an increase: There was a gradual increase in tourism.
  • a rise: We saw a sharp rise in electricity consumption.
  • a growth: The company reported a slight growth in revenue.
  • an upturn: The economy is showing a definite upturn.

Adjectives & Adverbs of Magnitude (How much?)

Combine these to describe the size of the change.

Adjectives: significant, substantial, considerable, dramatic, sharp, steep, slight, modest, gradual

Adverbs: significantly, substantially, considerably, dramatically, sharply, steeply, slightly, modestly, gradually

Example: There was a significant increase in demand. / Demand increased significantly.

2. Vocabulary for a Decrease

Verbs

These verbs are used to describe a downward movement in data.

  • decrease: Spending on food decreased over the period.
  • fall: The number of students fell from 200 to 150.
  • decline: Interest in traditional media has been declining.
  • drop: Unemployment rates dropped after the reform.
  • plummet: Stock prices plummeted following the announcement. (Dramatic)
  • plunge: The value of the currency plunged. (Dramatic)

Nouns

These nouns are used after articles or prepositions.

  • a decrease: The chart shows a decrease in the birth rate.
  • a fall: There was a slight fall in the average temperature.
  • a decline: The country is experiencing an economic decline.
  • a drop: A sharp drop in sales was reported.

Adjectives & Adverbs of Magnitude (How much?)

Use these to qualify the extent of the decrease.

Adjectives: significant, substantial, considerable, dramatic, sharp, steep, slight, modest, gradual

Adverbs: significantly, substantially, considerably, dramatically, sharply, steeply, slightly, modestly, gradually

Example: There was a sharp decline in profits. / Profits declined sharply.

3. Vocabulary for No Change or Fluctuations

Verbs

These verbs are used when data remains the same or moves up and down.

  • remain stable: The figures remained stable for three years.
  • level off: The rate of inflation leveled off in June.
  • remain constant: The number of users remained constant throughout the period.
  • fluctuate: The price of the product fluctuated wildly.

Nouns

These nouns describe periods of stability or change.

  • a period of stability: The chart shows a period of stability in the market.
  • a fluctuation: There was a considerable fluctuation in sales.
