IELTS Writing Task 1

Mastering Complex Sentences for a Higher Score

Key Complex Sentence Structures

★★★★★

Contrast Sentences (Using 'While' or 'Whereas')

These are essential for comparing and contrasting data trends. They show a clear relationship between two different pieces of information.

Example 1:

While the number of cars sold in Europe experienced a sharp decline, sales in Asia rose steadily over the same period.

Example 2:

The total expenditure on education increased, whereas the budget for healthcare saw a significant reduction.

★★★★☆

Time and Condition Sentences

These sentences are useful for describing a sequence of events or a cause-and-effect relationship based on time.

Example 1 (Time):

The population of the city grew substantially after the new university was built.

Example 2 (Condition):

The consumption of plastic bottles would likely decrease if the government introduced a recycling incentive.

★★★☆☆

Relative Clauses (Using 'which' or 'who')

Use these to add extra information about a noun (e.g., a city, a company, a person) without starting a new sentence.

Example 1:

The report, which was published in 2020, outlines a clear trend in global temperatures.

Example 2:

The average consumer, who is becoming more environmentally conscious, prefers products with minimal packaging.
