IELTS Writing Task 1 Vocabulary: Comparisons and Peaks/Lows

Vocabulary for Comparisons

Adjectives

  • Higher (than)
    The sales in 2020 were higher than those in 2019.
  • Lower (than)
    The unemployment rate was lower than the previous year.
  • More (than)
    The city attracted more tourists than expected.
  • Less (than)
    The company spent less on advertising than its competitors.
  • Similar (to)
    The trend in Europe was similar to that in Asia.
  • Equal (to)
    The production output was equal to last year's figures.
  • Identical (to)
    The results in both regions were identical to each other.
  • Comparable (to)
    The growth rate was comparable to that of the previous decade.

Adverbs

  • Comparatively
    The costs were comparatively lower in rural areas.
  • Relatively
    The increase was relatively modest compared to expectations.
  • Similarly
    Sales in both stores rose similarly over the period.
  • Equally
    Both products were equally popular among consumers.

Phrases

  • In comparison with
    In comparison with 2018, profits in 2019 were higher.
  • Compared to
    Sales dropped significantly compared to the previous quarter.
  • As opposed to
    The trend was declining, as opposed to the steady rise in 2020.
  • In contrast to
    In contrast to last year, production remained stable.
  • Outstripped
    The company's revenue outstripped its competitors in 2022.
  • Surpassed
    The number of visitors surpassed expectations in July.

Vocabulary for Peaks and Lows

Verbs

  • Peak (peaked, peaking)
    Sales peaked in December before declining.
  • Reach a high (reached, reaching)
    The stock price reached a high in mid-2021.
  • Hit a low (hit, hitting)
    Unemployment hit a low in 2020.
  • Bottom out (bottomed out, bottoming out)
    The market bottomed out after a prolonged decline.

Nouns

  • A peak
    The data showed a peak in production in 2019.
  • A high
    The company recorded a high in sales in July.
  • A low
    The chart indicates a low in temperatures in January.
  • A trough
    The economy experienced a trough during the recession.

Exemplary Sentences with Chinese Translations

More Than

  • The population of City A was significantly more than that of City B.
    A市的人口明显多于B市。
  • Online sales in 2023 were considerably higher than those in 2022.
    2023年的在线销售额远高于2022年。

Less Than

  • The cost of production was less than anticipated in the budget.
    生产成本低于预算预期。
  • The number of visitors was notably lower than the previous year.
    访客数量明显低于前一年。

Similarity

  • The growth patterns in both regions were strikingly similar to each other.
    两个地区的增长模式惊人地相似。
  • Export figures were comparable to those recorded in 2020.
    出口数据与2020年记录的数据相当。

Peak

  • Electricity consumption peaked during the summer months.
    电力消耗在夏季达到高峰。
  • The company’s profits reached a high in the third quarter of 2022.
    公司利润在2022年第三季度达到高点。

Trough

  • The economy hit a trough during the financial crisis in 2008.
    经济在2008年金融危机期间达到低谷。
  • Car sales bottomed out after a significant decline in demand.
    汽车销量在需求大幅下降后触底。
