IELTS Task 1: Flow Chart Essential Language & Techniques

Writing about processes requires specific grammatical structures and linking phrases to ensure the report is logical, cohesive, and accurate.

Core Techniques for Process Writing

1. Mandatory Passive Voice

Processes usually focus on the material or object (the receiver of the action), not the person or machine doing the action. Therefore, the Simple Present Passive voice is essential for high scores.

Active (Avoid): The factory pumps the water into the tank.

Passive (Use): The water is pumped into the tank.

2. Sequential Structure

You must describe the steps in the exact order they appear in the diagram. Use a variety of sequencers to connect the stages smoothly.

A. Sequential Linking Phrases (Sequencers)

These phrases help guide the reader through the sequence and improve the coherence of your report.

Function Expression Examples
Starting the Process The process commences when... / The cycle begins with... / The first step involves...
Moving to the Next Step Following this, the material... / Once X is complete... / Subsequently, Y is... / In the next stage... / At this point...
Showing Simultaneous Steps While X is being processed... / Simultaneously, another pipe... / At the same time...
Concluding the Process The final step is when... / Finally... / Ultimately... / The cycle concludes with...

B. Common Passive Process Verbs

Use these verbs, typically in the Simple Present Passive (e.g., is filtered, are separated), to describe the actions accurately.

Category Verb Examples (Used in Passive Voice)
Creation/Gathering is collected, is gathered, is extracted, is harvested
Preparation is filtered, is cleaned, is sorted, is separated, is crushed, is broken down
Modification is heated, is cooled, is mixed, is melted, is transferred, is pumped, is placed, is directed
Assembly/Completion is poured, is shaped, is checked, is packed, is distributed, is sold

C. General Process Vocabulary & Nouns

This list includes essential nouns and structural words needed to describe the overall diagram and the flow of materials.

Category Examples
Diagram Features (Overview) schematic, figure, stages, phases, cyclical, linear, entire procedure
Materials/Products raw materials, input, output, finished product, waste product, by-product, end product
Connecting/Housing Devices pipe, tube, conveyor belt, tank, chamber, boiler, container
Structural Linkers (Clauses) at this point, from where, after which, where (relative clause), which is then
